Burglary and Invasions

The map above shows the safest neighborhoods in St. Louis. While some areas may be statistically safer, home protection and knowledge are essential for everyone. The unlawful entry on one's property while occupants are inside is a Home invasion. The only difference between that and a Burglary is the absence of occupants. Being knowledgeable of both is very important. As such, we will discuss the most common ways intruders invade homes.

One of the first methods an intruder uses to enter a home is to check an unlocked door, window, or garage. A survey taken by YouGovAmerica showed that 7% of Americans don't lock their doors when leaving home, and 23% don't while they are present. Regardless of where you live, always lock any entrances to your home. This simple method may deter anyone from going forward with their scheme. Another method an intruder might use is to break in through a garage. Most garages lead right to the back of a home. All an intruder needs is a way to reach and pull the emergency latch installed at the top of most garages. Furthermore, to accomplish this task, all that is needed is a clothes hanger. As a result, this is an intruder's go-to route of entry. Let's assume both of these routes are well-secured. If that's the case, the next means of entry could be through a window via brick. This method may seem loud, but it's an almost guaranteed way to enter a home. Additionally, intruders have found numerous ways to lessen the sound when attempting this. The last method of intrusion we'll discuss is entering a place by knocking a door down. Most residential homes do not have steel doors or reinforced deadbolt locks. Thus, all an intruder needs to do is repeatedly target the door-hinge for the door to eventually open. As such, this method is one of the most used. This list does not cover all the possible routes an intruder can take to invade a home. However, the various methods mentioned are some of the most common.
